Semantic tags provide meaning to the structure of a webpage.It will tell what the content is

header: defines the header section of a webpage, usually containing the site title, main heading,logo, and navigation links.
nav: defines a section of the webpage that contains navigation links to other pages or sections within the same page.
main: defines the main content area of a webpage, which is unique to that page and
section: defines a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading, that can be used to organize related content within a webpage.
article: defines a self-contained piece of content that can be distributed or reused independently, such as a blog post, news article, or product description.
aside: defines content that is related to the main content but is not essential to its understanding, such as a sidebar or a callout box or advertisement.
footer: defines the footer section of a webpage, typically containing copyright information, contact details, and links to related pages or social media profiles.
