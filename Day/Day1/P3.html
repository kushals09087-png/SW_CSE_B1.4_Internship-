The Monkey and the CrocodileThe SettingOnce, in a forest, a sweet jamun (blackberry) tree grew right by the edge of a wide, flowing river. In the branches of this tree lived a clever, happy monkey who spent his days eating delicious fruit. In the river lived a crocodile. One day, the crocodile rested under the tree, and the friendly monkey tossed down some sweet berries. They quickly became the best of friends, sharing fruits and stories daily.The DeceitThe crocodile's wife eagerly ate the fruits her husband brought home. However, her greed soon took over. She realized the monkey ate these sweet berries every single day, meaning his heart must be even sweeter. She demanded that her husband bring her the monkey's heart to eat, or she would starve herself. The reluctant crocodile, torn between his wife and his friend, finally agreed.The TrapThe crocodile returned to the riverbank and invited the monkey to come across the water to meet his wife for a grand dinner. The monkey could not swim, so the kind crocodile offered to carry him on his back. As they reached the middle of the deep river, the guilty crocodile confessed his plan to kill him for his wife.The EscapeThe clever monkey did not panic. Instead, he smoothly lied to the crocodile: "My dear friend, I would gladly give you my heart! But I left it hanging on the branches of the jamun tree. We must go back so I can get it."Believing the monkey, the foolish crocodile immediately turned around and swam back to the riverbank. The moment his feet touched the shore, the monkey leaped high into the safety of his jamun tree. He looked down at the shocked crocodile and called out, "You foolish creature! How can a heart be separated from a body? Our friendship ends today. The Clever Rabbit and the Foolish LionThe TerrorA fierce, greedy lion named Bhasuraka ruled over a dense forest. He hunted ruthlessly, killing far more animals than he could ever eat. Terrified for their survival, the remaining forest animals gathered to make a desperate deal with the king. They promised to send exactly one animal to his den every single day for his meal if he agreed to stop his random slaughters. The lion accepted. The SacrificeOne day, the lot fell upon a small, helpless rabbit. As he walked toward his certain death, the rabbit decided he would not give up without a fight. He purposely dragged his feet and took long rests along the way. By the time he finally arrived at the lion's den, the sun was setting. The lion was starving, furious, and roaring in anger. The TrickThe lion demanded to know why the rabbit was so late. The tiny rabbit bowed low and squeaked out a clever excuse: "My king, it is not my fault! I was coming with five other rabbits, but another huge lion stopped us on the path. He claimed he was the true king of this forest. He ate the other rabbits, and I barely escaped to tell you!"The FallBlinded by absolute rage and pride, the lion demanded to face this challenger immediately. The rabbit led the furious beast deep into the woods to the edge of a deep, old well. "Look inside, Your Majesty," whispered the rabbit. "The imposter hides right down there."The lion peered over the edge and saw his own reflection in the still water. He mistook it for his rival. When he roared in anger, the well echoed his roar back at him. Believing the fake king was challenging him, thefoolish lion leaped deep into the well to fight. He crashed into the water and drowned instantly. The small rabbit hopped back to save the forest.The MoralWisdom and intelligence are far more powerful than mere physical strength.
